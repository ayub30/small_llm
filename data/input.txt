Q: What is a tax invoice?
A: A tax invoice is a document that includes the tax details of a transaction.

Q: What is the VAT threshold in the UK?
A: The VAT threshold in the UK is 90000 pounds per year.

Q: What is a sole trader?
A: A sole trader is a self employed person who owns and runs their own business.

Q: What is a limited company?
A: A limited company is a business structure where the company is a separate legal entity.

Q: What is corporation tax?
A: Corporation tax is a tax on the profits of limited companies in the UK.

Q: What is the corporation tax rate in the UK?
A: The main corporation tax rate in the UK is 25 percent.

Q: What is self assessment?
A: Self assessment is a system used by HMRC to collect income tax from self employed people.

Q: What is HMRC?
A: HMRC stands for His Majesty's Revenue and Customs and is the UK tax authority.

Q: What is a UTR number?
A: A UTR number is a unique taxpayer reference number used to identify a taxpayer.

Q: What is income tax?
A: Income tax is a tax on earnings paid to HMRC by individuals.

Q: What are the income tax bands in the UK?
A: The basic rate is 20 percent, the higher rate is 40 percent and the additional rate is 45 percent.

Q: What is national insurance?
A: National insurance is a tax on earnings that funds state benefits and the NHS.

Q: What is a P60?
A: A P60 is a document from an employer showing total pay and tax deducted for the year.

Q: What is a P45?
A: A P45 is a document given to an employee when they leave a job showing pay and tax details.

Q: What is payroll?
A: Payroll is the process of paying employees and calculating tax and national insurance deductions.

Q: What is PAYE?
A: PAYE stands for Pay As You Earn and is the system employers use to deduct tax from wages.

Q: What is a balance sheet?
A: A balance sheet is a financial statement showing assets, liabilities and equity at a point in time.

Q: What is a profit and loss statement?
A: A profit and loss statement shows revenue, costs and profit over a period of time.

Q: What is working capital?
A: Working capital is the difference between current assets and current liabilities.

Q: What is depreciation?
A: Depreciation is the reduction in value of an asset over time.

Q: What is an asset?
A: An asset is something of value owned by a business.

Q: What is a liability?
A: A liability is money owed by a business.

Q: What is equity?
A: Equity is the value of the business after liabilities are deducted from assets.

Q: What is a journal entry?
A: A journal entry is a record of a financial transaction in the accounting system.

Q: What is double entry bookkeeping?
A: Double entry bookkeeping records every transaction as both a debit and a credit.
